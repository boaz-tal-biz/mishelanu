**Notable gaps (full list in Section 4):**

- No `MISHELANU-KNOWLEDGE-BASE.md` exists in the repo (referenced in the brief).
- No email infrastructure at all — every outbound message goes through the simulated WhatsApp pathway. Privacy Policy implies an email channel that does not exist in code.
- No 404 / not-found / catch-all error page in the React app.
- No cookie banner or consent prompt anywhere, despite the Privacy Policy describing cookie use.
- Hardcoded follow-up message lives inside `client/src/pages/SimRequester.jsx` (a simulation screen) instead of in a server template — easy to miss when editing copy.
- "Mazel tov" (in code) vs "Mazal tov" (in `PROJECT-CONTEXT.md` brand voice) — inconsistent spelling.
- Backend ping-admin response and the matching frontend banner say similar things in different words — risk of drift.
- Hebrew transliteration register varies: "Mazel tov", "Yofi", "B'hatzlacha", "Toda raba", "Todah", "Kol hakavod", "Kol tuv", "Shalom", "B'ezrat Hashem", "Mishelanu" — copywriter should confirm a single style guide.
- Two parallel contact form surfaces (one on `/register` via `ContactBlock`, one on `/contact` via `ContactUs.jsx`) with overlapping but not identical strings — risk of drift.

## Section 4 — Gaps and inconsistencies

**Documents referenced in the brief but missing from the repo**

- `MISHELANU-KNOWLEDGE-BASE.md` was named in the audit brief — it does not exist in the repo. Context for this audit was taken from `PROJECT-CONTEXT.md` and `CLAUDE.md` instead.

**Surfaces that are described but not implemented**

- **No real email templates anywhere.** Every outbound message goes through the simulated WhatsApp pathway via `outbound_messages`. The Privacy Policy mentions admin sessions and refers users to `boaz@bless.network`, but no email-sending code exists. If email is part of the planned UX, the copywriter should know there is currently no email surface to fill.
- **No SMS templates.** Only WhatsApp simulation exists.
- **No 404 / not-found page.** `client/src/App.jsx` has no fallback `<Route path="*">` and the server's catch-all `app.get('*')` simply re-serves the React index. An unknown URL renders an empty page.
- **No cookie banner / consent prompt.** The Privacy Policy describes cookie usage (PRIV_Section_Cookies_Body_027) but no banner is rendered anywhere. Likely required pre-launch.
- **No alt text on the hero image other than "Mishelanu"** — same word three times across Header, Footer, Home. Acceptable, but a copywriter might want to vary.

**Duplicated / inconsistent strings**

- "From our community, for our community." appears twice — `client/src/pages/Home.jsx` and `client/src/components/Footer.jsx`. Single-source candidate.
- "Send Mishelanu a message" text recurs across the registration contact block, the application-expired link, and the contact-form heading.
- Two parallel contact forms with subtly different success messages:
  - REG_Contact_Success_066 (`Register.jsx` ContactBlock) → "**Toda raba** — Mishelanu has received your message and will be in touch soon."
  - CONT_Page_Success_H2_020 + Body_021 (`ContactUs.jsx`) → "**Todah!** Message received" / "Mishelanu will get back to you soon. In the meantime, shalom!"
  - CONT_Backend_Success_023 (`server/src/routes/contact.js`) → "Toda raba — Mishelanu has received your message and will be in touch soon."
  - All three say roughly the same thing but with different transliterations of "thank you" (Toda raba, Todah). Pick one.
- Ping-admin success path has two strings that don't quite match:
  - PROFM_Ping_Blocked_028 (frontend, after click) → "Admin has been notified — we'll review your profile shortly."
  - PROFM_PingAdmin_BackendOK_038 (server response) → "Mishelanu has been nudged — we will review your profile shortly."
  - The backend response is currently swallowed by the frontend; if the frontend ever surfaces it directly, two voices will appear.
- "Mazel tov" used in code (PROFM_LiveBanner_Body_002, WA_RequesterMatch_Body_003) vs "Mazal tov" in `PROJECT-CONTEXT.md` brand voice ("Mazal tov, Kol hakavod, Mensch..."). Reconcile.

**Hardcoded copy that lives in a surprising place**

- `WA_RequesterFollowup_Body_005` ("Glad Mishelanu could help! Save our number — next time you need something, just message us directly. Kol tuv!") is hardcoded in `client/src/pages/SimRequester.jsx`. In production this should come from the server. Today it's only visible inside the simulation screen — easy for a copywriter to miss.
- `WA_RequesterFollowup_LinkText_006` ("View their profile") and `WA_RequesterFollowup_SaveButton_007` ("Save Mishelanu") are also frontend-only sim chrome, not actual outbound message content.

**Conditional / branching copy that needs explicit choices**

- `WA_ProviderOpportunity_Body_001` includes optional lines (`{location_line}`, `{urgency_line}`, `{context_line}`) that render as blank lines when their source data is missing. Confirm whether to keep blank lines or collapse them.
- `PROFM_Deadline_Lapsed_Restarts_020` has a singular/plural variant. Currently "1 restart remaining" / "2 restarts remaining" are both formed by string concatenation.
- The Recommend page intro says "complete your recommendation within **48 hours**" while the actual window is 72 hours system-wide. This may be intentional (encourage urgency) but the copywriter should know.

**Hebrew transliteration register varies**

In active code: "Mazel tov", "Yofi", "B'hatzlacha", "Toda raba", "Todah", "Kol hakavod", "Kol tuv", "Shalom", "B'ezrat Hashem". `PROJECT-CONTEXT.md` lists "Mazal tov, Kol hakavod, Mensch, Baal HaBayit, L'chaim". Worth a single style choice (e.g. ashkenazi vs sephardi spellings) to apply across the audit.

**Strings whose audience is mixed**

- The home page's `Admin Login` button (HOME_Hero_AdminLink_014) is visible to anonymous visitors but only used by admins. Worth deciding whether this remains on a public homepage or moves behind a less-visible path.
- "Monitor" wording in `WA_SimProvider_EmptyState_009` ("Waiting for Mishelanu to send you a match from the Monitor.") leaks an internal product name to the provider audience.

**Ambiguous wording / TODO-flavoured items**

- "B'ezrat Hashem, connecting our community one recommendation at a time." (HOME_Hero_Blessing_013) — italic line on the home page. Decide whether religious framing belongs on an anonymous landing page or only inside post-registration flows.
- All three legal pages carry "Last updated: April 2026. Draft for prototype — subject to legal review before production launch." — strip or update before launch.
